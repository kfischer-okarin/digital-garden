χ2\chi^2χ2 Test

Source: https://www.scribbr.com/statistics/chi-square-goodness-of-fit/

χ2=∑(O−E)2E\chi^2=\sum{\frac{(O-E)^2}{E}}χ2=∑E(O−E)2​

Where:

  • χ2\chi^2χ2 is the chi-square test statistic
  • O is the observed frequency
  • E is the expected frequency

Goodness of Fit Test

You want to know if a sample of data which is a histogram of counts (independent variable is categorical, dependent variable is quantitative) conforms to an expected distribution of counts.

H0H_0H0​: The counts conform to the expectation

H1H_1H1​: The counts are statistically different

  1. Calculate the χ2\chi^2χ2 value as above
  2. Determine the degrees of freedom: number of groups/bins - 1
  3. Check the table below to see the probability that H0H_0H0​ must be rejected
