About the Tower of Babel

Source: Twitter thread by Ari Lamm

  • Common interpretations of the story include:
    • Temptation of hubris (pride)
    • Perils of technical innovation

What did the Babel builders do wrong?

  • Were they just a brotherly society of dedicated builders?
  • Were they becoming too proud for their own good? With the help of technology?
  • Did God need to destroy them because they were a threat?
  • The phrase used for "making bricks" (nilbena levenim) is only used one other time in the Bible
    • In Exodus when the pharao forces the Israelites to increase their brick output without giving them straw reference.bible.exodus.5 (Private)
    • Making bricks basically required slave labor in the ancient world
  • The word for "come" (as in "Come, let us...") (havah) - is like a forceful "Come on!", i.e. forcing something lesser in power to fulfill your wish as a ruler
    • Other uses are:
      • The pharao wanting to enslave the Israelites reference.bible.exodus.1 (Private)
      • Judah strongly demanding sex from a prostitute in Genesis 38
  • The "city fortified up to the skies" is similar in wording like the cities in Canaan that were built by wicked societies reference.bible.deuteronomy.9 (Private)
    • "impossible" (as in "nothing [...] will now be impossible") is actually also an allusion to the fortified cities (the Hebrew word yibbatzer)
  • "propose" (as in "nothing that they propose") has a connotation of plotting to pervert justice
  • So what is described in Genesis 11 is the birth of the first exploitative empire (The Akkadian Empire) in Mesopotamia
  • The single language was an enforced empire language - a regression from the diversity after the spreading out of the descendants of Noah in reference.bible.genesis.10 (Private)
  • Wanting to stay in one place (to solidify ones power) was a direct contradiction to God's command before after the flood "Be fruitful and multiply" in reference.bible.genesis.9 (Private)

Why did God punish them as He did?

  • To break up the exploitative society
  • In the end it was a blessing in disguise - to regain diversity

What's the message?

  • We should abhor injustice
  • We should embrace the goods of diversity
  • God is there as a good parent to guide our steps even if we fail at living up to those standards
